VoltStream Energy Efficiency Guide

VoltStream is a household smart energy usage monitoring platform. It helps users understand live power flow, solar contribution, smart device usage, historical trends, and billing impact.
VoltStream gives the user one place to track live energy flow, compare grid and solar usage, manage home devices, understand bill savings, and see eco impact.
VoltStream combines live monitoring, usage history, smart device control, billing insights, and assistant help in one platform.
VoltStream is designed to help a household use energy more clearly, reduce waste, improve solar-first usage, and make better day-to-day decisions about devices and bills.
Questions about this platform, this app, or this website should be understood as questions about VoltStream.
VoltStream works by combining live dashboard data, historical usage charts, smart device controls, billing insights, and assistant guidance in one place for the user.

Grid power is the live power drawn from the electricity grid.
VoltStream tracks the grid power your household is using, the devices contributing to grid usage, historical grid trends, and the billing impact of that grid usage.
Solar energy or solar power is the live power generated by the solar system and available for home usage.
VoltStream tracks solar energy generated by your household system, how much solar supports home usage, solar surplus, and the bill savings created by solar generation.
Energy balance is the difference between solar generation and grid draw.
VoltStream uses energy balance to show whether extra solar is available or whether extra grid power is needed.

Dashboard page:
The Dashboard page shows the live energy status chart, current grid power, current solar power, energy balance, bill savings, eco impact, usage by source, and top energy consumers.
The Dashboard is the best place to get a quick live picture of how the home is using energy right now.
Grid Power shows how much electricity is currently being taken from the grid.
Solar Power shows how much electricity is currently being generated by the solar setup.
Energy Balance shows whether solar is covering the load or whether extra grid power is still needed.
Bill Savings shows how solar usage is helping reduce the amount of electricity bought from the grid.
Eco Impact shows the environmental benefit of using cleaner energy, especially solar support.
Usage by Source helps the user compare how much of the selected period came from grid power versus solar support.
Top Energy Consumers helps the user identify which appliances are likely driving higher demand.

Usage History page:
The Usage History page shows historical grid consumption and solar generation. It supports daily, weekly, and monthly views so users can compare past energy trends.
The daily view is useful for checking what happened today and spotting heavier usage periods.
The weekly view is useful for comparing usage patterns across the week.
The monthly view is useful for understanding the larger bill trend and overall solar contribution.
The AI Summary on this page gives a simple explanation of what happened, how savings look, and what to do next.
The AI summary should be understood as helpful guidance based on the usage pattern, billing picture, and active devices.

Smart Control page:
The Smart Control page shows household smart devices, their ON/OFF state, current load, power usage, and controls for selected appliances.
The Smart Control page helps users manage household devices by room, device type, and running status.
Daily Essentials includes commonly used household devices such as fans, fridges, tube lights, and bulbs.
Climate Control includes higher-impact comfort devices such as AC units and heaters.
Custom or added devices may appear in Other Devices if they do not match the default grouped essentials.
If more devices in a group are running, the card glow becomes stronger to help users notice higher active usage.
Vacation Mode is meant for a low-usage or away setup and turns listed devices OFF.
Energy Saving mode is meant to keep essential devices running while reducing heavier or less necessary loads.
Summer Cooling and Winter Warm are comfort presets meant to favor seasonal devices.

Billing page:
The Billing page shows generated bill, payable bill, solar credit, budget status, invoice history, and whether solar savings or controlled grid usage is keeping the user in budget.
Generated Bill means the total bill created before solar savings or credits reduce it.
Payable Bill means the amount left to pay after solar savings are taken into account.
Solar Credit means value gained because solar reduced the need to purchase energy from the grid.
Budget Status helps the user understand whether the home is currently staying within the selected monthly target.
The invoice list provides recent billing records and downloadable demo invoice PDFs for review.

Assistant page:
The Assistant page lets users ask questions about VoltStream, grid power, solar energy, solar surplus, smart devices, billing, and platform navigation.
The Chat Bot is best for general energy questions, solar savings, grid usage, and simple energy explanations.
The Q&A Bot is best for VoltStream-specific guidance based on the VoltStream guide, including platform pages, billing views, controls, and platform usage.
If a user asks a broad VoltStream platform question, the assistant should still answer with a short platform overview before guiding them to a more specific page or feature when needed.
The Quick Assistant is a smaller floating helper for fast questions without leaving the current page.

Recommended solar panel angle:
For this VoltStream training demo, the recommended fixed panel angle for maximum solar output is about 15 to 20 degrees, facing south where possible. To get better solar support in VoltStream, keep the tilt close to the local latitude and make sure the panels stay free from shade during peak sunlight hours.

Solar savings:
Solar generation should be used by the home first. Every kWh generated by solar reduces the need to buy energy from the grid. In VoltStream, stronger solar-first usage usually means better bill savings because less electricity has to be purchased from the grid.

Grid and solar interpretation:
If solar generation is greater than home demand, extra solar can be treated as surplus or sent back to the grid depending on the metering setup. If grid draw is high, heavy appliances like water heaters, AC units, washing machines, microwaves, and cookers are common contributors.

Smart device recommendations:
Turn off high-wattage devices when not required. Move flexible loads like washing machines and charging to sunny hours to use more solar power. Energy-saving mode should keep essential devices on and turn heavy appliances off.

Historical usage:
Historical grid and solar data helps identify peak usage periods, compare daily or monthly patterns, and understand how much solar generation offsets grid consumption.
If the user wants to improve savings, historical usage should be checked together with device activity and billing trends.
A stronger solar period combined with lower heavy-device overlap usually improves savings.
If the user sees repeated high grid periods, heavy devices should be moved to sunnier hours when possible.

Nomenclature:
Grid Power or Grid Draw means the live power currently being taken from the electricity grid.
Solar Power or Solar Generation means the live power currently generated by the solar panels and available for home usage.
Solar Surplus means extra solar power left after the home usage is covered. Depending on the meter setup, this can be sent back to the grid or counted as excess generation.
Energy Balance or Net Usage means the difference between grid draw and solar generation. If solar is higher than demand, the app shows extra solar sent back. If demand is higher, the app shows extra grid power needed.
kW means kilowatt. It is a live power value at one moment in time, such as 2.4 kW grid draw right now.
kWh means kilowatt-hour. It is total energy used or generated over time, such as 84 kWh this week.
Solar Coverage means the percentage of grid usage that solar generation helped offset in the selected period.
Bill Savings means estimated money saved because solar energy reduced grid energy purchase.
Peak Grid Period means the day, week, or month where grid usage was highest in the selected history view.
Generated Bill means the bill amount before solar savings reduce the final payable amount.
Payable Bill means the amount the user is expected to pay after solar savings or solar credit are applied.
Eco Impact means the environmental benefit of the user's energy choices, often shown through reduced CO2 impact.
RAG means Retrieval-Augmented Generation. The app first retrieves relevant context from the energy guide, then uses that context to answer.
A chunk is a smaller section of the source document used for retrieval.
An embedding vector is a numerical representation of text meaning, used to compare how similar a question is to document chunks.
A grounded answer is an answer generated only from the provided VoltStream context.
An out-of-scope question is a question outside VoltStream, solar, grid usage, devices, or billing. The bot should respond with: I don't have that information.

Out-of-scope policy:
If a user asks about a topic outside VoltStream, solar energy, grid usage, device energy consumption, billing, or the provided energy guide, answer: I don't have that information.
